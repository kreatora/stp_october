About Us

Our research group focuses on studying sustainability transitions across various sectors and regions. We aim to understand the complex dynamics of socio-technical systems and how they can be steered towards more sustainable configurations.

Our interdisciplinary team combines expertise from environmental sciences, sociology, economics, and policy studies to develop comprehensive frameworks for analyzing and facilitating sustainability transitions.

The National Research Infrastructure for Energy project (NFDI4Energy) is a consortium under the umbrella of the National Research Data Infrastructure (NFDI) dedicated to building an Open Ecosystem for FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) Research Artefacts in Interdisciplinary Energy Systems Research. Within NFDI4Energy, STP generates and prepares energy data that can be used by researchers in Germany and elsewhere. This includes data on technology support policies and climate and energy targets, but also on regulations for where which technologies (e.g. wind farms, power lines) can and cannot be built. Long data series for such data are not presently available, and the individual datasets (e.g. for a specific country or set of countries) are generally not machine-readable or in a format which can be compared across countries.
